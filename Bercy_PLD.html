CHLOE BERCY

Name : Programmable Logic Design Project

Course : Honors Digital Electronics

Date : March 2, 2020

In this project, I used a programmable logic device to build a message display on a seven segment display. The display had a total of 16 states with 4 inputs that read "3 notifications". I created an online simulation of the circuit on MultiSim. This circuit was downloaded to a PLD, which is connected to a breadboard and MyDAQ with a seven segment display. This circuit has four 7-bit counters to decrease the frequency of the clock voltage provided by CMOD S6, AOI logic to determine when each segment on the SSN should be high, and probes to simulate the display of the SSN. The biggest problem faced was while transferring the circuit simulation to the PLD. The PLD didn't seem to be working the way it should, and I realized that there is a difference between the clock input used for simulation vs. breadboarding and needed to find how much to slow down this clock input provided by CMOD S6. To see my work, check out the documentation.
